About This Work

This collection represents over a decade of creative exploration — a body of work that spans disciplines, media and intentions. Each piece is the result of deliberate choices made at the intersection of intuition and craft, where the process of making is inseparable from the meaning of the outcome.

The works gathered here are not ordered by date or medium, but by the questions they ask. Some are quiet; others are disruptive. All of them are honest. They reflect the conditions under which they were made and the conversations they were trying to enter.

Approach and Method

Every project begins with sustained observation — of a place, a material, an idea, or a relationship between things. This phase of looking and listening is unhurried. It cannot be compressed. What emerges from it is not a concept but a direction: a way of moving into the work that allows the unexpected to remain possible.

Execution follows its own logic. Techniques are chosen not for their familiarity but for their fitness — what the work needs, not what habit prescribes. This means returning often to unfamiliar ground, and accepting the discomfort that comes with it as a sign of genuine engagement rather than inadequacy.

The job of the artist is always to deepen the mystery — not to explain it, not to resolve it, but to make it more fully present to those who are willing to sit with it.

Francis Bacon
